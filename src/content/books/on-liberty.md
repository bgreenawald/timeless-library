  that can be legitimately exercised by society over the individual. Mill
  articulates his famous 'harm principle,' arguing that the only purpose for
  restricting liberty is to prevent harm to others. The text vigorously defends
  freedom of speech and individuality while warning against the social and
  political dangers of the 'tyranny of the majority'."
